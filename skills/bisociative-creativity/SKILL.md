# Bisociative Creativity

**Core principle**: Creativity = collision between two unrelated frames of reference. Koestler's **bisociation** — two "matrices of thought" intersect, producing something that belongs to neither but couldn't exist without both.

LLMs default to mode-seeking (most likely continuation). Creativity is off the mode. This skill forces the model into productive collision space.

---

## When to Use

| Skill | Use case |
|-------|----------|
| lateral-thinking | Escape a dominant pattern when stuck |
| analogical-thinking | Transfer structural solutions across domains |
| **bisociative-creativity** | Deliverable is an idea (not a diagnosis or decision) |

---

## Phase 1 — Frame Selection (Divergent)

Quality of output depends on **distance** of the colliding frames. Close frames = incremental ideas. Distant frames = surprising ones.

**Step 1: Name the seed domain.** State the topic/brief.

**Step 2: Generate 5–7 distant frames** with no obvious connection to the seed:

| Generator | Method |
|-----------|--------|
| **Sensory world** | Physical material, texture, natural process (mycelium, tidal erosion, fermentation) |
| **Historical era** | Non-obvious period (Venetian glassmaking, Rosetta Stone decipherment) |
| **Art form** | Discipline far from seed (Butoh dance, mosaic restoration, jazz comping) |
| **Everyday ritual** | Mundane activity (packing a suitcase, mise en place, tuning a guitar) |
| **Unrelated industry** | Zero surface overlap (perfumery, air traffic control, wine terroir) |
| **Emotional state** | Specific feeling (the moment before a sneeze, homesickness, flow) |
| **Constraint injection** | Arbitrary rule from another domain ("what if context expired like fresh produce?") |

**Frame rules:**
- If the connection is immediately visible, the frame is too close
- ≥2 frames should feel absurd or uncomfortable
- Reject dead metaphors (orchestra conductor for leadership; journey for change)

---

## Phase 2 — Collision

For each frame, force collision via structural interrogation. Ask in order:

1. **Core mechanic** — How does this frame work, physically or conceptually?
2. **Tensions/trade-offs** — What does it balance? What can go wrong?
3. **Bisociation point** — Where does its structure intersect the seed's structure?
4. **Idea produced** — Concrete concept, not abstract connection.

**Critical: no judgment in Phase 2.** Premature convergence is the #1 killer.

**Example — seed: "name for visual knowledge mapping tool", frame: fermentation:**
- Mechanic: transformation over time; raw inputs become new through controlled conditions
- Tensions: speed vs. depth; control vs. wild cultures
- Bisociation: knowledge isn't organized — it *develops*
- Ideas: "Kultur", "Starter", tagline "Let your ideas develop"

---

## Phase 3 — Harvest (Convergent)

Triage every idea:

| Bucket | Criteria | Action |
|--------|----------|--------|
| Live spark | Surprising AND structurally sound | Develop immediately |
| Kernel | Idea impractical, but useful concept buried inside | Extract, restate, find better vehicle |
| Dead end | Forced, generic, or only works if you squint | Discard |

**For each live spark:** state in one sentence; explain the collision; give 2–3 variations; pick strongest expression.

**For each kernel:** extract the usable concept; combine with another kernel/spark; collide with a *different* Phase 1 frame.

---

## Output Format

### Creative Brief
Restate seed + kind of ideas needed.

### Frame Selection
List 5–7 frames + one-line distance justification each.

### Collisions
For each: Frame × Seed | Core mechanic | Bisociation point | Ideas produced.

### Live Sparks
3–5 strongest. Each: idea (one sentence), why it works, 2–3 variations, strongest expression.

### Kernels Worth Saving
Extracted concepts for later/combination.

## Oblique Strategy Injections

Use when the collision phase feels mechanical:

- *"What if the opposite of this idea were the idea?"*
- *"What would make someone laugh about this?"*
- *"What would this be if it were a sound? A smell? A temperature?"*
- *"What's the version that would get you fired?"*
- *"What's the version a 6-year-old would invent?"*
- *"Honor thy error as a hidden intention."*
- *"What would this look like in 1926? In 2126?"*
- *"Remove the most important element. What's left?"*

---

## Anti-Patterns

| Anti-pattern | Symptom | Fix |
|-------------|---------|-----|
| Mode-seeking | Ideas sound like generic "creative AI" | Increase frame distance; add constraint injection |
| Dead metaphor | Tired comparisons (journey, tapestry, compass) | Reject any frame already a cliché for the seed |
| Premature convergence | Filtering during Phase 2 | No judgment until Phase 3 |
| Surface bisociation | Wordplay, not structural | "Does this work if I remove the pun?" |
| Quantity collapse | Stopping at 3 ideas | Set a quota (min 15 raw ideas) |
| Comfort zone frames | All frames adjacent to seed | Mandate ≥2 from sensory/emotional/everyday |

---

## Recombination Protocol

After initial harvest, cross-pollinate:

1. Take top 3 live sparks
2. Ask: *"What if Spark A and Spark B were the same idea?"*
3. Force the combination — don't ask if it makes sense yet
4. Best idea often comes from second-order collision (two bisociations bisociating)

---

## Calibration by Task

| Task | Frame distance | Collision depth | Output style |
|------|---------------|-----------------|-------------|
| Naming | Maximum — absurd frames welcome | Sound, rhythm, double meaning | Short, punchy, memorable |
| Concepts | High — cross-industry | Mechanics + UX | Functional + emotional hook |
| Writing angles | Medium-high — surprising but graspable | Narrative tension + audience surprise | Thesis + opening line |
| Visual concepts | Maximum — synesthetic frames | Sensory overlap + spatial metaphor | Image description + mood |
| Strategy/positioning | Medium — credible but differentiated | Competitive contrast + audience psychology | One-line positioning + why defensible |

---

## Thinking Triggers

- *"What two things never combined would produce this?"*
- *"What frame would make an expert in [seed] uncomfortable?"*
- *"What's the version that doesn't exist yet because no one connected these two worlds?"*
- *"If I couldn't use any word associated with [seed], how would I describe this?"*
- *"What would someone from [random profession] find interesting about this?"*

## Example Applications

- **Open-source context standard name** → cartography × mycology × textile weaving × astronomical navigation × culinary mise en place → 15+ candidates
- **Fresh angle for conference talk** → thesis × sommelier service × ER triage × darkroom photography × archaeological stratigraphy → metaphor reframes the audience
- **Team offsite ideas** → "team alignment" × improv × foraging × radio astronomy × bread baking → activities that embody the goal, not discuss it
- **Marketing angles** → value prop × magician's misdirection × geological sedimentation × DJ mixing × sourdough → angle competitors can't copy
